Take a Step Back to DVDs

Timothy Hill May 16, 2025

It is time for a digital detox! 

Imagine a world where we aren't bound to the whims of a streaming service or digital store; a world where our media is really ours, not just a license to be taken away at the whim of these services! Help me take back ownership of the games and movies we pay for!

Disclaimer

This is gonna be a heavy opinion based post with facts within the article, please understand there is nothing wrong with disagreeing.

Why?

It is my opinion that we as a community should attempt a digital detox from digital media services when we can and opt into physical methods. I am writing this call to action to reduce digital media and to reduce subscription based content. Let us go back to owning a copy, owning our media, to where no one can take it away.

What is right with digital?

I will not be the first to say, that digital media is convenient, you don't have to worry about the disk, cartridge, or whatever Nintendo may call it. So long as you remember your login information you don't need to worry about losing your game/movies through hardware problems like water, scratches, or anything else of that nature. This is because these streaming and gaming services have backups for backups so that you are able to have access to games that they allow you to have. This also saves on space with the clunky physical media. Before people would have bookshelves with games/movies, but now not so much. 

What is wrong with digital?

In most cases, the service that is holding onto your digital media can decide to close down or remove that single piece of media removing your ability to access it again. I feel like the idea of us buying the product but then always having the risk of this product being taken away just because the distribution service is like, "na, you're not having it anymore".

Small Exceptions

There is a very rare case where even if the media is removed from the store, you can still redownload it as many times as you wish, with that rare case being steampowered.com. GOG.com is another digital media source that also allows you to download and redownload the game if it's removed from their site, the main difference between steam and GOG is that if steam disappears, your games are gone. If GOG disappears your installer still works so long as you have the offline installer.

Tho I don't agree with digital media, steam is doing it somewhat right with GOG having future proof the issue.

It forces users to potentially pay more

Subscription based services have been in play for a long time and are not new. However, there are 200+ subscription based services for movies and TV shows alone. For the common services (Disney, Netflix, and Amazon) for the "with ads" service you are paying a total of 26.97$ a month just to watch what you want to watch. Tho this may be cheap, keep in mind that once you no longer wish to pay for a subscription, you no longer have access to this media.

I have searched for TV shows like Summer Camp Island, Wolfboy and the Anything Factory, and The Dragon Prince. All of these are streaming services exclusive OR bootleg purchasable. With one no longer available in the US anymore. Personally, I want to support my movie and TV show creators but not as much as these subscription services that are more than willing to just say, "No more of this content". 

But wait...

Before you say it, I understand that if Netflix comes out with their Netflix original and has a DVD version that I am still supporting Netflix. however the idea that I outright buying physical media over paying for their subscription service shows my stance and what I like.

A New Issue Arises

Nintendo officially confirmed on April 2, 2025, that the Switch 2 cartridge will only contain authorization codes for the game, and not the game itself. This is very shaky grounds on what ownership you really have. Originally you owned the game and were able to share it without monetary gain, then turned into you own a license of a game with restrictions as per the licensing agreement, and now with the new setup for Nintendo, you are renting the game now and will lose rights to them if/when they deem it necessary.

Do What You Can

I noted that we should attempt to remove these services from our lives. However, I myself understand that its very difficult to just stop all of these services. They are everywhere and in no hurry to stop. So what I want to do is avoid it if you can. Go for DVDs, Go for Disk games, Go for GOG and download offline! Do what you can, and if you cant, let it be known that your not happy with subscription based services!

THIS IS NOT A CALL TO PIRATE

Pirating has its place, but this is not one of them. I want you to support creators and I want you to pay for these games. What this is a call for is a call to avoid digital services when you can.

My Opinion

As the title suggests, I want us all to take a step back and go back to DVDs, CDs, cartages even offline digital downloads. It may be clunky but at the same time, this is the only true way to have your game and keep it too.
