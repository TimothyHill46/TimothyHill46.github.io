Helluva Boss 

Timothy Hill May 13, 2025

Disclaimer

1. I am disregarding my religious background when reviewing this series. 

2. I am disregarding my political background when reviewing this series.

3. some spoilers are to be expected

4. this review is only in the context of all the episodes up to season 2 episode 12 and the 4 shorts listed within the helluva boss playlist, totaling 24 videos to watch. New episodes will be added as standalone reviews when it comes.

Helluva Boss 

The show is about 3 imps and a hellhound: Blitzo (the o is silent), Moxxie, Millie, and Loona! Blitzo got tired of being a basic nobody and decided to start his own business. "what is his business?" you might ask, hunting down and killing in any specified way that dammed souls may request/demand, and who do he and his business partners hunt? Humans on Earth!

How Does Blitzo Get To Earth?

Blitzo does a transaction, for lack of better words, with a powerful demon known as an ars goetia named Stolas. Stolas has a book with magical powers to open portals to the "human world." Through this book, he is meant to study the stars and understand prophecies. However, through some "favors," Stolas is willing to give up his sacred book at risk of great punishment.

Anything Else You Should Know?

In almost every episode there can be bloody bodies, some form of killing, swearing, and a LOT of sexual jokes. They even show (tho blurred) dildos in 2 of the episodes. This series is very crude, more so than Hazbin Hotel, so be warned. 

The Good

This series is actually hilarious. The writing is well done (to a point and I will get to that), the characters are loveable, and, in a lot of aspects, are relatable when it comes to their emotional issues. When watching the series you get to grow with and watch the characters progress in their emotional state, from the petty drama between Blitzo and Moxxie to the romantic married couple between Moxxie and Milly. 

The Bad

Right around late season 2, the story got shaky, facing similar issues as to Hazbin Hotel. Mostly with the questionable pacing of the characters' emotional status. It is apparent that since the 2nd episode, Stolas did not have a good relationship with his daughter due to family issues. However, in the later episode of season 2, we have a major falling out that is caused by questionable reasons and felt very forced. A few other events took place as well which also feels like context is missing or out of order, more info on that in my in-depth post (to be added).

I think one of the major flaws with this storyline is that at many points that it feel rushed. It’s not nearly as bad as Hazbin Hotel, you start to notice it in episodes like season 1 episode 6 truth seekers. by no means a bad episode, but it felt like the flow could of been smoother as it felt like bits of the story were taken out due to keeping it within the appropriate time limit.

Lastly, we have very little Milly growth, now the writers tried to combat that with showing story about her and her family, but even then Milly feels like a flat character, for being one of the major characters in the series she has less growth then the "I say, I say!" salesmen.

The Rating 

So of all the harshness I gave, this series is unmatched when compared to many other indie series or even standard TV shows, I hope the writing gets better and this is just time constrained issue that we will not have in the future. As it sits right now, I am giving this series a 7/10. A major step above Hazbin, as it has incredible music and pretty good writing for most of the story. All the music is amazingly placed and works well with the story. I just hope that season 3 does not fall into the same pit holes as Hazbin Hotel.
